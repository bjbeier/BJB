My Journey into Linux and Docker

Working in tech for half of my life and tinkering in tech for almost all of it, I've dabbled in a little bit of everything. Recently, I decided it was time to move my home lab away from standalone heavy VMs and into the world of containerization.

Why Docker?
The efficiency gains were immediate. Instead of dedicating 4GB of RAM to a VM running a simple DNS blocker, I could run Pi-Hole in a container using a fraction of the resources.

The learning curve wasn't flat—understanding volumes and networking took a weekend of "trial by fire"—but the result is a portable, clean infrastructure defined entirely in code (YAML).
